<td valign="top"><pre>Below is an explanatory overview of CPO (Co-Packaged Optics) and a breakdown of the core investment names in the space.
1. What is CPO (Co-Packaged Optics)?
Technical definition:
 CPO stands for Co-Packaged Optics.
In a traditional AI data center, the compute chip (such as an Nvidia GPU or switching chip) and the optical module (which converts electrical signals into optical signals for transmission) are physically separate. The optical module is plugged into the front panel of the switch like a USB drive. The electrical signal has to travel through a long copper trace from the chip before it reaches the optical module.
What pain points does it solve?
 As AI models become larger and larger, data transmission speeds are racing from 800G to 1.6T and even 3.2T. At those speeds, the traditional “disaggregated pluggable” model runs into physical limits:
1. Exploding power consumption: When electrical signals travel such a long distance through copper, the loss is enormous and power consumption becomes staggering. 
2. Signal degradation (and latency): The higher the speed, the more prone long-distance electrical transmission is to errors. 
3. Insufficient panel space: There is simply not enough room on the front panel of a switch to fit that many densely packed optical modules. 
The revolutionary change brought by CPO:
 CPO directly packages the optical engine (the core part of the optical module) together with the compute chip (such as the switching chip) in the same socket, or even on the same substrate. This shortens the distance for electro-optical conversion from more than ten centimeters to just a few millimeters.
That dramatically reduces power consumption and lowers latency, making it the ultimate networking solution for future ultra-large AI compute clusters in the 3.2T-and-beyond era.

2. Breakdown of the CPO value chain and comparison of core investment names
 The CPO space will not have just one winner; it is a complex ecosystem. Below are the five most important U.S.-listed companies in the current CPO space and a comparison of their valuations, grouped by role in the value chain:
The rule-makers and brains: switching chip / DSP giants
 These two companies not only make chips, they also shape CPO architecture itself, because the optical components must be packaged around their switching chips.
1. AVGO (Broadcom)
- Role: The undisputed global leader in switching chips (the Tomahawk series). It is the most aggressive commercial promoter of CPO, and its Bailly CPO switch has already begun shipping to top cloud customers.

The optical component and technology leaders: the dual champions of lasers and optical modules
