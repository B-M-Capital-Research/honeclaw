# Gold Market Drivers Framework

Gold (XAU/USD) is both a safe-haven asset and a non-yielding asset, so its price is driven by the following core factors:

## 1. Macro Factors

- **Real yields**: Gold is negatively correlated with U.S. real yields, such as the 10-year TIPS yield. Higher rates raise the opportunity cost of holding gold and are bearish for gold prices.
- **USD exchange rate**: Gold is priced in U.S. dollars and usually has a negative correlation with the Dollar Index (DXY).
- **Inflation levels (CPI / PCE)**: Gold is seen as an inflation hedge. Prices tend to be supported when the market expects inflation to stay elevated for a long time and central banks react slowly.

## 2. Safe-Haven Demand

- **Geopolitical risk**: War, sanctions, or geopolitical conflict often push capital into gold.
- **Financial-system risk**: Demand for gold rises when banking crises or credit-default risk increases.

## 3. Supply and Demand

- **Central-bank buying**: Accumulation by central banks, especially in emerging markets, is a strong long-term tailwind for gold.
- **Gold ETF inflows**: Watching position changes in large ETFs such as GLD can reveal institutional capital flows.

## 4. Technical Indicators

- **Key price levels**: Historical highs and round numbers such as $2000 or $2500
- **Trend analysis**: The 200-day moving average (SMA) is often used as the bull / bear dividing line
